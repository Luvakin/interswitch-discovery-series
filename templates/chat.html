Based on the document, there are two main cognitive biases discussed:

1. Anchoring Bias: This is when people rely heavily on the first piece of information they encounter. In UX design, this means users often make decisions based on the first option they see.

2. Confirmation Bias: People tend to search for and favor information that confirms their existing beliefs. This affects how users interact with interfaces and process new information.

These biases are crucial for UX designers to understand because they help predict user behavior and create more intuitive interfaces.

Goal

The goal of this discussion is to explore the factors, models, and principles that guide human decision-making and their application in UX design to improve user engagement and satisfaction.

Introduction

Human decision-making is a complex cognitive process that influences every aspect of life, including how users interact with digital interfaces. In UX design, understanding how people analyze, evaluate, and decide helps create intuitive, user-friendly experiences.

Key Concepts

  • Cognitive Biases: Mental shortcuts that influence decision-making (e.g., anchoring bias and confirmation bias)
  • Emotions: Emotional states can override rational thinking and sway decisions
  • User Experience Applications: How these concepts apply to creating better digital interfaces
  • Decision-Making Models: Frameworks for understanding how people make choices
